The QA layer behind
Lilly Chat.

We launched the Lilly workflow in December 2025 as a chat-only operation — and layered on a dedicated QA team from the start, to ensure every patient conversation met the same clinical, compliance, and experience standard from the very first ticket.

How the standard was built

At launch, we reviewed tickets that patients themselves had rated as good and balanced that against our training detail on what good looks like. From both, we built a QA framework on five pillars:

Accuracy & Compliance Risk Prevention Communication & CX System Use & Documentation Professional Conduct
How we sample

5–15 tickets per agent per week depending on tenure, plus 100% review of every DSAT. A blend of random, targeted, thematic, and risk-driven selection — fairness for agents, priority coverage where patient safety lives. It lets us zero in on what really matters, where it matters.

What it enables

Our QA layer is one of the primary reasons CSAT has held above 90% consistently — and it gives new hires the review density they need to ramp fast. The volume of sampling lets us maintain a maximum 1:20 QA-to-agent ratio.

Why a dedicated team

This depth can't ride along with team leadership. TLs already carry attendance, adherence, real-time feedback and coaching, escalations, and admin — 1,400+ full-depth reviews a month requires its own function, or it simply doesn't happen at this rigor.

Not isolated checkmarks — a story

Every week, the QA team collates trends and insights across all reviews and feeds them to the training team. General knowledge gaps become targeted retraining before they become patterns — the reviews don't just score the past, they steer the next month.

The safety floor — automatic 0, flagged & escalated
Medical advice given (dosage, injection timing, clinical recommendations)
Adverse event, product quality complaint, or OSI missed or mishandled
Incorrect storage guidance (freezing, ignoring time/temperature rules)
Refill or early-fill rules misapplied
Safety event not documented, or documented in the wrong place
Wrong routing that could delay safety / compliance handling
Rude, dismissive, or misleading communication about safe use
Procedure bypass on a safety-critical flow

These never earn partial credit. Every one of the 1,421 reviewed tickets is a documented checkpoint that safety events were caught, logged, and routed correctly — your compliance posture, operationalized.

04 · Where this goes next

Chat is covered. Voice is next.

The system works. Now the program is about to outgrow the team running it.

Everything in this deck — the 94.9% composite, the 90%+ CSAT, the weekly trend-to-training loop — is delivered by 8 QA reviewers doing full-depth manual reviews. With Lilly Voice launching and total agent count heading past 500, the same standard needs more hands to hold it.

Inside your roadmap, not beside it. We know GiftHealth has its own QA roadmap, and nothing here is a proposal to build outside of it. What we're showing is the system that's already working — and where we can plug it into your plans. Whatever direction that roadmap takes, one thing holds today: as long as reviews stay manual at this depth, coverage scales with people.

Bring the proven framework — pillars, sampling discipline, 100% DSAT review, safety floor — to Lilly Voice the same way it works with Chat.

Scale

Align

Co-plan with your QA team so our layer supports and accelerates your plans rather than duplicating them:

  • Hugo QA feeds your roadmap insights on what's working in the current framework
  • If useful, extend Hugo QA coverage to other vendors
